A curated list of resources I regularly recommend to students, mentees, and
early-career economists. If you have suggestions, please
[reach out](mailto:jemo44t@gmail.com).

## Start Here

- **Book:** _The Third Door: The Wild Quest to Uncover How the World's Most
  Successful People Launched Their Careers_ by Alex Banayan
- **Beginner's guide:** Dr Matt Clancy's
  [Guide to #EconTwitter](https://mattclancy.medium.com/a-beginners-guide-to-econtwitter-d237a3a4608b/)
- **For African students applying to graduate school:** the
  [Graduate Applications International Network (GAIN)](http://gain-learning.net/) —
  start with this advice
  [here](https://riccardodicato.wordpress.com/2021/04/23/phd_application_tips/)

## Programming, Data &amp; Economics

- [CME 193 — Scientific Python (Stanford)](http://web.stanford.edu/class/cme193/syllabus.htmll)
- [QuantEcon.jl](https://quantecon.org/quantecon-jl/)
- [UCLA Institute for Digital Research and Education — Stata](https://stats.idre.ucla.edu/stata/)
- [Innovations for Poverty Action — Coding Guide](https://povertyaction.github.io/guides/cleaning/readme/)

## Writing &amp; Research Practice

- [Professional Etiquette for the Mature Economist](https://www.ime.usp.br/~abe/lista/pdfWU48lyxCJK.pdf)
  by Daniel S. Hamermesh
- Prof. Chris Blattman —
  [How to write like a Mad Man](https://chrisblattman.com/2012/02/12/write-like-a-mad-man/) ·
  [How to write an essay](https://chrisblattman.com/2010/02/17/how-to-write-an-essay/)
- Prof. John H. Cochrane —
  [Advice on writing economic papers (PDF)](https://www.fma.org/assets/docs/membercontent/writing_cochrane.pdf)
- Prof. Don Davis —
  [Advice on choosing a research topic](http://www.columbia.edu/~drd28/Thesis%20Research.pdf)
- Christine Cai —
  [Recent Advances in Applied Micro Methods (PDF)](/applied_micro_methods.pdf)
